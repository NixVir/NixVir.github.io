We've added a new Sports Betting Activity section to the [Nixvir Dashboard](/dashboard.html), tracking gaming metrics as a competing discretionary spend indicator for the ski industry.

**Why track sports betting?** A significant market for new skiers and snowboarders is adult males ages 18-35. To the extent that some of these individuals are more focused on sports betting than in the past, it may reduce their discretionary income and therefore reduce their participation frequencyâ€”or their likelihood of picking up skiing or snowboarding at all.

The new section includes:

- **U.S. Trailing 12-Month Handle** - Total dollars wagered across all legal U.S. states over the past year
- **U.S. Latest Month** - Most recent monthly sports betting handle, with seasonality following major sports calendars (NFL, NBA, MLB)
- **Ontario Annual Handle** - Canada's largest sports betting market, which launched regulated iGaming in April 2022
- **Monthly Handle Trend Chart** - Visualizing the rapid growth trajectory since 2019
- **Annual U.S. Handle Totals** - Year-over-year comparisons showing market expansion

In 2019, only a handful of states had legal sports betting. By 2024, 38 states plus D.C. were operational, and the market continues to expand rapidly.

Data sourced from [Sports Handle](https://sportshandle.com/sports-betting-revenue/) (U.S.) and [iGaming Ontario](https://igamingontario.ca) (Canada).
